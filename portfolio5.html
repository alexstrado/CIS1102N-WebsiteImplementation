Comparative Study On Different Types of Motherboards

You can think of a computer's motherboard as a complex printed circuit board (PCB) or in simpler terms its the computer's nervous system that connects all of the different components of a PC, such as the ram and cpu. The motherboard allows for different extensions and additions like storage, sys fans, cpu fans, and a graphics card. It is the most important part of a computer since in the first place without it you cant assemble a computer, its size also matters since different sizes allows for different physical components and such. These different sizes are referred to as “form factors” a form factor is a standard specification of a motherboard and its compatibility with different physical parts, psu, and the case.

The motherboard's size and different form factors were made out of the need of different uses and cases such as better heat management, more expandability, and ease of upgradeability. The major form factors are AT, ATX, BTX, Extended-ATX, LPX/NPX, Micro-ATX, Mini-ATX, Pico BTX, and Standard-ATX. Majority of consumer motherboards fit into either Standard-ATX, Micro-ATX, and Mini-ATX. LPX and NLX form factors are usually used for mass produced systems such as business desktops they are significantly thinner and are usually “low profile” or “slim” they are largely considered obsolete since they were replaced by both Micro-ATX and Mini-ATX’s such as the Intel Nuc which USC uses for their computer lab due to their smaller size and better thermals.
